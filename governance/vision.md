# Vision

## What we are building

A demonstrative web application that enables organizations to manage their accounts receivable intelligently — scoring each overdue account for recoverability, prioritizing collection effort by expected value, and generating professional, personalized communication drafts for each case.

The system serves two user profiles simultaneously: the **collections operator** who works the daily priority queue case by case, and the **executive** who monitors portfolio health and asks analytical questions in natural language.

All functionality is demonstrated over fully synthetic datasets, making the application a safe, compelling sales asset for InterWare's AI practice.

## Who it serves

| Profile | Need |
|---|---|
| InterWare AI practice | A concrete, deployable demo that elevates sales conversations from "what is AI" to "here is AI solving your collections problem" |
| Collections operator | A daily prioritized queue with AI-drafted communications ready to review and send |
| Executive / CFO | Real-time portfolio health view with natural-language query capability |
| Prospect organizations | Confidence that AI can be applied to a measurable, present pain (stranded working capital) with proportional investment |

## Why this domain

Accounts receivable recovery appeals to **loss aversion** — the psychological principle that recovering capital already considered lost drives stronger executive action than promises of new revenue. This maximizes the demonstrative asset's commercial impact across sectors. The Pareto principle further validates the approach: a small subset of overdue accounts concentrates most of the recoverable value, making intelligent prioritization directly profitable.

## Measurable outcomes the system must achieve

| Outcome | Observable signal |
|---|---|

## Principles (§)

**§1 — Human defines, machine executes.**  
All strategic decisions (scope, architecture, prioritization, approval to send communications) belong to a human. Rai proposes; the team decides.

**§2 — Stop on defects.**  
A failing test, a broken type, an ambiguous requirement — these pause the work. They do not get papered over and deferred.

**§3 — Demonstrative scope is a feature, not a constraint.**  
The system is not a product. It is evidence. Complexity that does not serve the demonstration is waste. Every feature must trace to a scenario a prospect will see.

**§4 — Dignity by design.**  
Communication drafts generated by the system must be professional, proportionate, and respectful. The system must make it structurally difficult to send a message that would be considered abusive or coercive.

**§5 — Model agnosticism.**  
No part of the system's business logic couples to a single AI provider. All LLM calls route through OpenRouter. Swapping a model must require a config change, not a code change.

**§6 — Synthetic-only data.**  
No real customer, invoice, or organizational data enters the system at any stage of development or demonstration.
